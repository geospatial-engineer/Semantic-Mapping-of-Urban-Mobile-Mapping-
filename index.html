Overview

This project bridges visual semantics and 3D urban geometry by projecting OCR detections from equirectangular panoramas onto LiDAR facades and fusing repeated observations across views.

Abstract

The framework detects storefront text and business identifiers in panoramic imagery, converts these detections into viewing rays, intersects them with LiDAR-supported facade geometry, and consolidates repeated detections into stable semantic markers. The result is a searchable semantic layer over urban point clouds suitable for digital twins, inventories, and urban information systems.

Highlights

Deterministic and interpretable geometric localization; deep-learning-based OCR extraction; multi-view fusion of repeated detections; semantic querying by facility type, keyword, and brand name; validation on two urban mobile mapping datasets.

Method Workflow

The processing chain follows four major stages from OCR extraction to semantic fusion and interactive retrieval.

OCR Extraction

CRAFT and EasyOCR detect text regions in panoramas and recover OCR tokens, confidence values, and bounding boxes.

Semantic Parsing

Rule-based taxonomy logic converts raw OCR tokens into facility categories, service keywords, and business names.

Geometric Projection

Bounding-box corners are mapped from equirectangular pixels to 3D rays and intersected with facade-supporting LiDAR geometry.

Fusion and Query

Multi-view detections are clustered into stable markers, enabling interactive semantic search inside the 3D point cloud.

Datasets and Experiments

Two urban experiments evaluate transferability across imaging systems and acquisition contexts.

Experiment 1 — Enschede, Netherlands

CycloMedia panoramic imagery and mobile mapping LiDAR were used to validate OCR-based semantic extraction, geometric facade localization, and multi-view fusion in a Dutch urban street scene.

Experiment 2 — U.S. Urban Dataset

A Maverick mobile mapping dataset with a Ladybug panoramic camera was used to assess the framework under a different imaging configuration and urban acquisition setting.
